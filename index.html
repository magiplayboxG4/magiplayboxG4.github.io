Our Mission

Our mission is to provide education, technical and professional training in the digital sector to young underprivileged people by leveraging their potential and willpower. We endeavour to truly develop their employability which will allow them and their families to escape poverty in a sustainable way, and contribute to the social and economic development of their countries.

Our beneficiaries are typically underprivileged or disadvantaged, in extremely precarious situations, according to the criteria PN has defined/specified for each of the countries in which it operates.

PN’s objective is that at least 90% of the students enrolled in our programs can escape poverty, be professionally more successful relatively to the national average, and take advantage of a ‘Gateway for Life’.

PN’s additional objective is to reduce inequality between genders. Therefore, PN strives to enrol a ratio of at least 50% girls (or a maximum of two thirds) at each of our centers.

A Better Selection

View

SELECTION TEAM INCREASES EFFICIENCY

For the first time, clickers (individual electronic boxes), acquired in 2012, thanks to a grant from the French Embassy in the Philippines. It has a double objective for PN: to ensure a better reliability of the results and maximize the trips of the team while reducing the associated costs. Thanks to the usage of those clickers, exam results were announced 10 to 15 minutes after the end of the exam, which avoid “losing” any student between the written exam and the motivation interview. It is a major improvement for PN because in the past years, we had to contact by phone or social media for the selected candidates. It was a laborious logistic for the teams since some of the candidates were unreachable.

  • Date - 2014
  • Total Scholars - 49
  • View - www.passerellesnumeriques.org

A New Home

A CENTER FOR THE SECOND YEAR STUDENTS

The main change in students’ lives in 2018 in the Philippines was the change of center 2. After six years in Elizabeth Place, PN wanted to find a more spacious and better-situated location. Located close to center 1 and to the University of San Carlos (USC), there is a kitchen, a dining area, a common hall, an educator’s office, twelve student bedrooms, one room for the volunteers, and a big outdoor activity area. The computer laboratory (previously in USC) was also moved there, thanks to the support from the alumni (through their Solidarity Act donations).

What We Do?

Information Session

Information sessions to explain the IT sector and its opportunities, and to introduce our programs.

Written Examination

Written exams in logic, mathematics and English to check the applicants’ abilities.

Motivational Interview

Individual interviews to assess each applicant’s motivation, maturity and mindset.

Social Investigation

Social investigations to evaluate the family’s resources and validate their eligibility. Only the most underprivileged are admitted.

Contract signing

The finalist of scholars together with their parents/guardians will sign the memorandum of agreement.
